Korean Ingredients Guide

Discover the characteristics and storage methods for jang (fermented sauces), namul (vegetables), and grains that define Korean cuisine. Find accessible substitutes and practical tips for cooking abroad.

Ingredients that Create Korean Flavors

Jang, namul, and bap (rice) are the foundation of Korean cuisine, but they require careful selection and management. Understanding each ingredient's characteristics and proper storage will make your home cooking even more delicious.

Jang & Fermented Foods

Fermented jang is the heart of umami flavor, created through time and dedication.

üçØ Doenjang (Soybean Paste)

  • Characteristics: Savory paste made from fermented meju (soybean blocks)
  • Uses: Jjigae (stew), muchim (seasoned dishes), seasoning sauce
  • Storage: Refrigerate, minimize air exposure
  • Tip: Recommend traditional meju doenjang without additives
  • Substitute: Chickpea miso, white miso

üå∂Ô∏è Gochujang (Red Chili Paste)

  • Characteristics: Sweet, salty, and spicy paste made from gochugaru (chili flakes) and glutinous rice
  • Uses: Stir-fry, muchim, bibimbap, seasoning
  • Storage: Store in airtight container in refrigerator
  • Tip: Check sugar content before selecting
  • Substitute: Mix gochugaru + soy sauce + sugar

ü´ò Ganjang (Soy Sauce)

  • Characteristics: Basic seasoning fermented from meju and brine
  • Types: Guk-ganjang (light flavor), jin-ganjang (dark flavor)
  • Storage: Keep in cool place away from direct sunlight
  • Tip: Check salt content and choose by intended use
  • Substitute: Tamari soy sauce (gluten-free)

üßÑ Garlic & Ginger

  • Characteristics: Essential aromatics in Korean cuisine
  • Uses: Seasoning, broth, jjigae, stir-fry
  • Storage: Whole garlic at room temperature, minced garlic refrigerated
  • Tip: Store minced garlic in chamgireum (sesame oil)
  • Substitute: Use ginger powder when garlic unavailable

Namul & Vegetables

Seasonal namul is all about fresh aroma and texture.

ü•¨ Sigeumchi (Spinach)

  • Characteristics: Spring namul rich in vitamins and iron
  • Preparation: Blanch and rinse in cold water to maintain color
  • Storage: Can freeze after blanching (3 months)
  • Tip: Squeeze out water thoroughly before using
  • Substitute: Swiss chard, kale (blanch)

ü•ï Kongnamul (Soybean Sprouts)

  • Characteristics: Bean sprouts with crisp texture
  • Preparation: Boil with lid closed to remove fishy smell
  • Storage: Freshness is key, use quickly after purchase
  • Tip: Cook immediately after blanching
  • Substitute: Sukju namul (mung bean sprouts) for quick cooking

üåø Gosari & Chwi Namul (Bracken & Aster)

  • Characteristics: Dried namul with deep, earthy flavor
  • Preparation: Soak in water then boil to remove bitterness
  • Storage: Store dried in airtight container (room temp 6 months)
  • Tip: Soak sufficiently in cold water
  • Substitute: Spinach, minari (water dropwort) for fresh vegetables

ü•í Mu & Baechu (Radish & Napa Cabbage)

  • Characteristics: Essential vegetables for kimchi and soup dishes
  • Uses: Kimchi, mu namul, soup, broth
  • Storage: Refrigerate (wrap in plastic or newspaper)
  • Tip: Use mu with skin on for broth
  • Substitute: Western napa cabbage

Bap & Grains

Master grain combinations and storage to dramatically improve your rice.

üåæ Baekmi & Hyeonmi (White Rice & Brown Rice)

  • Baekmi: Soft texture, easy to digest
  • Hyeonmi: Rich in fiber and nutrients
  • Blend: Recommend 70% white rice + 30% brown rice
  • Storage: Airtight container, refrigerate (prevent bugs)
  • Tip: Rinse until water runs clear

üçö Japgok (Mixed Grains)

  • Types: Barley, oats, sorghum, black beans, soybeans
  • Nutrition: Rich in protein, fiber, vitamins
  • Preparation: Pre-soak and mix when cooking rice
  • Storage: Airtight container, refrigerate
  • Tip: Adjust water for texture

ü•¢ Gim & Dashima (Seaweed & Kelp)

  • Characteristics: Sea vegetables that add ocean aroma
  • Uses: Gim (side dish, rice balls), dashima (broth)
  • Storage: Keep in airtight container away from moisture
  • Tip: Soak dashima in water for 30 minutes for broth
  • Substitute: Parae instead of gim, shiitake instead of dashima

ü´ò Dubu & Kong (Tofu & Beans)

  • Characteristics: Premier plant-based protein source
  • Uses: Jjigae, muchim, jorim (braised), stir-fry
  • Storage: Keep tofu submerged in water in fridge (2-3 days)
  • Tip: Soak beans and cook with rice
  • Substitute: Tempeh, lentils (vegan protein)

üõí Shopping & Storage Tips

Freshness is crucial for ingredients. For vegan or halal-certified products, always check the product labels. Frozen namul should be thoroughly thawed and moisture removed before use.

  • Look for 'Korean style' markings at Asian markets or online shops
  • Choose jang products without additives or organic options
  • Use seasonal namul, frozen namul also works well
  • Store grains in airtight containers in refrigerator to maintain freshness
  • Adjust cooking methods and seasoning when using substitutes
