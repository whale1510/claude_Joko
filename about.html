Joko's Jang-Namul-Bap Project

A learning-focused recipe archive that makes jang (fermentation), namul (vegetables), and bap (grains)—the core elements of Korean cuisine—accessible to everyone.

We aim to rediscover the value of local ingredients and traditional cooking methods at today's table. Understanding the combination and balance of basic ingredients makes Korean cuisine much easier and more approachable. The Joko team creates recipes, guides, and educational materials to share these discoveries with many people.

Project Goals

  • Organize the core elements that constitute the fundamental flavors of Korean cuisine in an easy-to-understand way.
  • Introduce substitutions that consider diverse dietary requirements such as vegan and halal.
  • Provide a modular recipe structure that can be used for learning and teaching.

Planning & Production

This project started from a study group based on Korean cooking practice classes. Recipes and guides are co-created by instructors and student teams who conduct cooking classes, and are improved each season by incorporating on-site feedback.
