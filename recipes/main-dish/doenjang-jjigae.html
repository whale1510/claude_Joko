Doenjang Jjigae

The essence of Korean home cooking - savory doenjang with seasonal vegetables and tofu. Once you master the basic broth and ingredient prep, you can create deep flavors anytime.

Serving Notes

Create a visual presentation that brings out the rich, savory aroma.

  • Serve in a ttukbaegi (earthenware pot) or ceramic bowl to keep it warm longer.
  • Top with shiitake mushrooms and green onions to enhance the fragrance.
  • Pair with multigrain rice and fresh kimchi for a balanced meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons doenjang (Korean soybean paste)
  • 3 cups anchovy-kelp broth
  • 1 potato, 1/2 zucchini, 1/2 onion
  • 1/2 block tofu
  • 1 cheongyang chili pepper, 1/2 green onion
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic

Vegan & Halal Substitutions

  • Broth: Replace with vegetable broth made from dried kelp and shiitake mushrooms
  • Doenjang: Use vegan-certified or halal-certified soybean paste
  • Spice level: Use bell pepper or jalape√±o instead of cheongyang chili
  • Added protein: Add vegan soft tofu or chickpeas

Instructions

  1. Pour broth into a pot and while it heats, cut potato, zucchini, and onion into bite-sized pieces.
  2. When broth starts boiling, dissolve doenjang through a strainer to evenly distribute the paste.
  3. Add potato, zucchini, and onion, then simmer over medium heat for 7 minutes until tender.
  4. Add tofu, cheongyang chili, and minced garlic, then simmer for 3 more minutes and check seasoning.
  5. Finally, add green onion, turn off heat, cover with lid and let it steam for 1 minute.
