Crisp and light Bean Sprout Namul is a basic side dish that goes well with any Korean meal.

Texture Tips

Remember these points to maintain the crispness of bean sprouts.

  • Rinse in ice water immediately after blanching to preserve texture.
  • Remove as much moisture as possible to prevent diluting the seasoning.
  • Add ground perilla seeds for nuttiness and thickness.

Ingredients

  • 300g bean sprouts
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp chopped scallions
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Substitution Tips

  • Use perilla oil or toasted sesame oil instead of sesame oil for a nutty aroma.
  • Add a bit of soy sauce instead of salt for umami flavor.
  • Thinly sliced paprika or jalape√±o can replace Korean green chili peppers.

Instructions

  1. Rinse bean sprouts thoroughly, blanch in boiling water for 2 minutes, then rinse in cold water.
  2. Drain in a colander and gently press by hand to remove remaining moisture.
  3. In a bowl, mix bean sprouts with minced garlic, chopped scallions, salt, and pepper lightly.
  4. Add sesame oil and toasted sesame seeds, mix well, then transfer to a serving plate.
