Korea's essential side dish, made from salted and fermented Napa cabbage seasoned with chili powder and various aromatics.

Cooking Tips

Tips for making delicious Kimchi.

  • Use fresh ingredients for the best flavor.
  • Adjust seasoning to your taste preference.
  • Serve immediately for optimal taste and texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 napa cabbage
  • Coarse sea salt (for salting cabbage)
  • 1/2 radish
  • A handful of green onions
  • 4‚Äì5 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 cup Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 2 tbsp salted shrimp (saeujeot)
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sugar

Substitution Tips

  • Vegan substitution: Replace salted shrimp with vegetarian fish sauce made from mushrooms or fruit, or simply use salt. Remove regular fish sauce.
  • Halal substitution: Use halal-certified fish sauce or salt instead of salted shrimp or regular fish sauce. Make sure to also check other ingredients like sugar and chili powder for halal compliance.
  • Allergy substitution (focus on soy, wheat, shellfish, eggs): For shellfish allergies, replace salted shrimp or fish sauce with salt or vegetarian fish sauce.

Instructions

  1. Cut the cabbage into quarters and sprinkle coarse salt between each layer. Let it sit for 2‚Äì3 hours, flipping halfway.
  2. Rinse the salted cabbage several times in cold water and drain well.
  3. Julienne the radish and cut the green onions into 5 cm lengths. Mix garlic, ginger, red pepper flakes, salted shrimp, fish sauce (optional), and sugar to make the kimchi paste.
  4. Spread the seasoning and radish/green onion mixture evenly between the cabbage leaves.
  5. Store in a container, ferment at room temperature for 1‚Äì2 days, then refrigerate.
